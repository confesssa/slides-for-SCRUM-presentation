What is Scrum?

Scrum - is a framework that helps teams work together. Scrum describes a set of meetings, tools, and roles that work in concert to help teams structure and manage their work.

Who is involved?

Product Owner

  • Developing and explicitly communicating the Product Goal
  • Creating and clearly communicating Product Backlog items
  • Ordering Product Backlog items
  • Ensuring that the Product Backlog is transparent, visible and understood

Scrum Master

  • Helping the product owner maintain the product backlog
  • Helping the team to determine the definition of done for the product and remove impediments
  • Educating key stakeholders on Agile and Scrum principles
  • Coaching self-organization and cross-functionality within the team

Developers

  • Creating a plan for the Sprint, the Sprint Backlog
  • Instilling quality by adhering to a Definition of Done
  • Adapting their plan each day toward the Sprint Goal
  • Holding each other accountable as professionals

How it works?

Sprints - are the heartbeat of Scrum, where ideas are turned into value.

Sprint based on the following events

  • Sprint Planning
  • Daily Scrum
  • Sprint Review
  • Sprint Retrospective

Sprint planning

  • The team decides on a goal for the sprint based on the priorities set by the product owner
  • The team then select the product backlog items they believe should be done in the sprint
  • The developers identify the detailed work (tasks) required to complete those product backlog items

Daily Scrum

  • "What did I complete yesterday that contributed to the team meeting our sprint goal?"
  • "What do I plan to complete today to contribute to the team meeting our sprint goal?"
  • "Do I see any impediment that could prevent me or the team from meeting our sprint goal?"

Sprint Review

  • reviews the work that was completed and the planned work that was not completed
  • presents the completed work to the stakeholders
  • collaborates with the stakeholders on what to work on next

Sprint Retrospective

  • reflects on the past sprint
  • identifies and agrees on process improvement actions
  • three main questions arise in the sprint retrospective:
    • What went well during the sprint?
    • What did not go well?
    • What could be improved for better productivity in the next sprint?

What about tools?

Product Backlog : it contains an ordered list of product requirements that a scrum team maintains for a product. Common formats include user stories and use cases.

Sprint Backlog : it is composed of the Sprint Goal (why), the set of Product Backlog items selected for the Sprint (what), as well as an actionable plan for delivering the Increment (how).

Scrumban : it is a tool for the visualization of the current work of the Scrum team. The Scrum board has three columns: to-do, in progress, done. Each task gradually moves from column to column

Increment : it is a ready-to-use part of a product that has to be realized by the end of a sprint
